Job Focus (Restoration versus Optimization)

ASSUMPTIONS

  • 12 month average rate (per well) used as basis for dollar per barrel estimate.
  • For wells that produced for less than 12 months in 2018, forecasts obtained using 17.5% annual decline to obtain 12 month average.
  • Annualized Average is based on 2018 uplift only and contains no forecasts.

OBSERVATIONS

  • On a cost per bbl basis, based on the first twelve month production uplift, both rigless activities @ $3.5/bbl & rig-based activities @ $8.6/bbl are value adding.
  • Within rigless jobs, tubing wash jobs added the highest value in comparison to spend.
  • Repayment Arrears (RA) jobs added higher value than routine
  • NAOC selects better candidates for RA activities
  • Better performance seen in production restoration jobs than optimization activities

RECOMMENDATION

  • NAOC should pursue more Rigless activities.
  • When level change is the goal, Rigless level change methods should be considered first before considering overall recompletions with rig.
  • These Rigless activities should be aimed more at Production Restoration than optimization
  • Better selection process required for production optimization.
  • Strategically, OER should focus on WBUR to identify good restoration candidates.
